Wow, retirement anniversary number two.\u00a0 It was the first Sunday of May, 2013 that we concluded our pastoral ministry and entered into retirement.\u00a0 In our case, we retired to travel and the very next day we drove off with our RV, starting the next primary chapter of our lives.<\/p>\n

This last year has been terrific.\u00a0 We traveled from Houston to the northwestern corner <\/a>of the continental United States and then journeyed at a leisurely pace south along the western coast where we enjoyed amazing scenery and cool Pacific Ocean temperatures.\u00a0 We visited numerous national parks and, in general, had a blast.<\/p>\n

Our winter and early spring has been spent doing a variation of our fulltime RVing lifestyle.\u00a0 We\u2019ve volunteered at the Texas San Jacinto Battleground\/Battleship Texas State Historic Park<\/a>.\u00a0 In exchange for donating 100 hours of our time each month we\u2019ve enjoyed \u201cfree parking.\u201d\u00a0 That \u201ctrade\u201d has saved us some serious, and needed, cash! We\u2019ve thoroughly enjoyed this experience which allowed us to do some very interesting things while being close to family and friends.\u00a0 While we\u2019re more than ready to begin our travels again we enjoyed the volunteering experience enough that we\u2019ve already signed up to do it again next year.<\/p>\n

I did a few more clergy-like things than I did our first year but not a lot.\u00a0 I filled for our pastor when he was away, filled in for our Sunday School teacher (who happens to be my son) when he was away, did a baby dedication, and finalized my series of books of devotionals<\/a>.\u00a0 Aside from that I\u2019ve happily sat in the pew, appreciating the ministry of others.<\/p>\n

All in all, we\u2019ve spent over 4 months in the Houston area during this stay.\u00a0 That means we suspended our \u201cchurch hopping<\/a>\u201d ways and settled into a more typical church attendance routine.\u00a0 While visiting many different churches during our travels is enjoyable we\u2019ve missed the sense of community associated with being a part of a congregation.\u00a0 This being our second winter as part of our home church helped us feel more a part of things.\u00a0 It\u2019s interesting to me how things we at first felt were somehow different become, in just a few weeks, just \u201cthe way it is done here.\u201d\u00a0 One thing that become increasingly clear is that no church can be evaluated in just a week or two.\u00a0 Churches have personalities and that personality isn\u2019t apparent until one is part of the congregation (and involved beyond an hour on Sunday mornings) for a while.\u00a0 Don\u2019t get me wrong: I enjoy visiting churches, but I know that being a regular, contributing part of a church\u00a0family is superior.<\/span><\/p>\n

So, I\u2019d say retirement is going quite well, thank you!\u00a0 We\u2019re well aware that we are blessed to live this life and we don\u2019t take it for granted.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
