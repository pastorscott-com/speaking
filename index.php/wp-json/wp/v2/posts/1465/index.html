It’s been awhile since I’ve heard this song on the radio but for some reason it’s come to mind a couple of times lately.\u00a0 It comes from David Baroni’s “Promised Land” project and it’s called “Took me out of Egypt.”\u00a0 The message of the song is powerful.\u00a0 The singer thanks God for freedom from the old way of life – “Egypt” yet finds another level of slavery, this on the inside. I love the words of this song and these are, I think at the heart of the matter:<\/p>\n

Lord You took me out of Egypt
\nNow take Egypt out of me
\nYou delivered me from Pharaoh now set me free from me
\nLet my heart become a promised land
\nWhere the desert used to be
\nLord You took me out of Egypt
\nNow take Egypt out of me<\/em><\/p>\n

Thank God for his deliverance from the old way of life, it’s a transformation that makes all things new.\u00a0 However, in that delivered life, over time, the believer is dismayed to find that something of the old way remains.\u00a0 There’s competition for the Lordship of one’s life.\u00a0 Will it be God’s way or mine?\u00a0 This song describes the believer coming to a decision.\u00a0 Self must bow to the Lordship of Christ.\u00a0 The prayer then, is “Lord, you took me out of the old life but something of that old life remains in me.\u00a0 Now, I ask that you will do a deeper work in my heart that I might be not only free from the consequences of my sin, but from the nature of sin and self.\u00a0 Take the throne of my heart.”<\/p>\n

I love the message of this song and even more, I love what the Lord does in the lives of those who come to that place of full surrender.<\/p>\n
